STALKER 2 Has Gone Gold

  • The highly anticipated STALKER 2: Heart of Chornobyl has gone gold, confirming its release after a lengthy development time.
  • The game's realism and immersive atmosphere are emphasized in the new trailer, along with pre-order bonuses like usable in-game items.
  • Players can explore a vast open-world full of dangers and choices in Chernobyl's Exclusion Zone when STALKER 2 releases on November 20, 2024.

Fans patiently waiting for the release of STALKER 2: Heart of Chornobyl won't have to wait much longer, as the game has finally gone gold, signaling the final stretch before its release. The November 20, 2024 release date of STALKER 2 will mark the end of a lengthy development saga for the game, marked by multiple delays.

The original STALKER: Shadow of Chernobyl game from 2007 took inspiration from classic Soviet-era science fiction stories, setting the game in the "Exclusion Zone" surrounding the Ukrainian city of Chernobyl, which was depopulated by a nuclear reactor meltdown in 1986. Featuring realistic gunplay, challenging survival mechanics, and a tense, apocalyptic atmosphere, STALKER became a beloved classic among shooter fans, and arguably one of the earliest modern open-world survival games. Unfortunately for fans eager to see a sequel, STALKER 2's development path has been a long, bumpy road, marked by delays, the dissolution and reformation of developer GSC Game World itself, and the outbreak of war between Ukraine and Russia. The saga was even the subject of a documentary film called War Game: The Making of STALKER 2, focused on the series' history and the effects of the war on staff at GSC Game World.

S.T.A.L.K.E.R. 2: Heart of Chornobyl

S.T.A.L.K.E.R. 2: Heart of Chornobyl is a sequel to the award-winning game franchise developed by GSC Game World. Experience one-of-a-kind gameplay, featuring the elements of a first-person shooter, immersive sim, and horror. The Chornobyl Exclusion Zone is a unique, dangerous and ever-changing environment. It promises a lot — the artifacts of unbelievable value can be yours if you dare to claim them. The price you may pay, on the other hand, is no less than your own life.
