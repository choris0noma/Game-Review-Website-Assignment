Battlefield 2042 Reveals Patch Notes for September 2024 Update

  • Battlefield 2042's next update on September 10 will introduce new modes and content, as well as fix minor issues.
  • Update 8.1.0 brings Breakthrough UH-60 and Control Unlocked modes, ribbons, which will let players earn ribbons faster and unlock a new helicopter.
  • While DICE continues to support Battlefield 2042, EA has begun teasing the next game in the series.

The next Battlefield 2042 update will be released on September 10. The upcoming Battlefield 2042 update will introduce new content and minor changes to the game, continuing DICE’s support after the game no longer has seasonal content.

Originally released in 2021, Battlefield 2042 is the latest entry in the Battlefield franchise. At launch, Battlefield 2042 received negative and mixed reviews, with both critics and gamers disappointed with several technical issues, and some calling it a “step back” when compared to its predecessors. Despite failing to meet both financial and critical success at release, EA and DICE continued to support Battlefield 2042, and the game has greatly improved since then. In the last three years, Battlefield 2042 had seven seasons, ending with “Turning Point,” which was released on March 19, 2024.

Battlefield 2042

Battlefield 2042 is a 2021 first-person shooter, developed by DICE and published by Electronic Arts. The twelfth main installment in the Battlefield series, it was released on November 19, 2021, for PlayStation 4, PlayStation 5, Windows, Xbox One, and Xbox Series X/S. 2042 is the first installment since 2006's Battlefield 2142 to solely be a multiplayer video game, without a single-player campaign. It is also the first installment to feature support for cross-platform play.

The game received mixed reviews from critics and a more negative reception from players for its technical issues, lack of features, and certain changes to the gameplay. The game failed to meet the sales expectations of Electronic Arts. A new Battlefield installment, under the head of Motive Studio, is in development.
